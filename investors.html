The Problem

Fragmented AI Costs

Small businesses spend $500-5,000/month across ChatGPT Team, Claude, Midjourney, Jasper, and other AI subscriptions. Annual costs reach $18,000-60,000 with unpredictable pricing increases.

Data Privacy Concerns

Regulated industries (legal, healthcare, finance) hesitate to use cloud AI due to compliance risks. Client data, financial records, and proprietary information sent to third-party providers.

Vendor Lock-In

API rate limits, service downtime, and platform changes prevent businesses from scaling. No control over models, features, or roadmap. Technical teams required for integration.

Key Risks & Mitigation

We've identified and planned for the major challenges

Risk: GPU Supply Chain Constraints

NVIDIA GPU shortages or price spikes could increase COGS and delay shipments.

Mitigation:
  • Pre-purchase 3-6 months of GPU inventory using raise funds
  • Negotiate volume pricing with distributors
  • Maintain relationships with 3+ GPU suppliers
  • Design flexibility: support RTX 4000 and 6000 series

Risk: Cloud AI Price Wars

OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google could slash prices, making cloud AI cheaper than on-prem hardware.

Mitigation:
  • Focus on privacy/compliance customers (not price-sensitive)
  • Emphasize control, customization, and no vendor lock-in
  • Recurring revenue model means customers already committed
  • ROI still positive even with 50% cloud price drops

Risk: Technical Complexity

Customers struggle with setup, maintenance, or troubleshooting despite "turnkey" promise.

Mitigation:
  • White-glove onboarding for all customers (included in price)
  • Aigent Hub remote diagnostics and patching
  • Comprehensive documentation and video tutorials
  • 24/7 support with 2-8hr SLA depending on tier

Risk: Slow Sales Cycles

SMBs take 6-12 months to evaluate, delaying revenue and extending runway.

Mitigation:
  • Target pain-driven buyers (compliance deadlines, cloud cost overruns)
  • Offer 30-day money-back guarantee to reduce perceived risk
  • Pilot program: 5-10 units at cost for testimonials
  • Industry association co-marketing for credibility

Risk: Open-Source Competition

Technically savvy customers could DIY with Ollama, Docker, and open-source tools.

Mitigation:
  • Target non-technical buyers (lawyers, doctors, accountants)
  • Value-add: agent marketplace, remote management, support
  • OpenClaw orchestration is proprietary and difficult to replicate
  • Time-to-value: 30 minutes vs 2-4 weeks DIY

Risk: Key Person Dependency

Founder Quenten Beaux holds most technical and business knowledge.

Mitigation:
  • Hire VP Engineering and VP Sales in first 6 months
  • Document all processes, workflows, and technical architecture
  • Build cross-functional team (no single point of failure)
  • Key-person life insurance ($2M coverage)
